The Snow Leopard

The Snow Leopard is critically endangered, with an estimated population of between 4,000 and 6,000 individuals in the wild. The Snow Leopard populations have declined by over 20% in the last three generations due to loss of their natural habitat, illegal hunting, and fragmentation of their habitat.

Humpback Whale

There are several populations of humpback whales in the world, each with a different size. For example, the population of North Atlantic humpback whales is estimated to be around 350 individuals, while the population of Indian Ocean humpback whales is estimated to be around 12,000 individuals. These estimates may vary based on the latest data and research.
